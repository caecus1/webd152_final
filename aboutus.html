Our History

In 1950, George Alcott Walker was stationed at the naval base in Point Loma. While there, he fell in love with the natural beauty the Southern California coastline had to offer, and made plans in a letter to his then fiancee and future wife Abigail to settle somewhere near the coast someday.

That day came in 1953, when the newlyweds scouted a scenic but otherwise not very profitable tract of land with a simple cottage very much in need of repairs. WIth George's handy-work knowledge and Abigail's love of gardening, they made a lovely home for themselves.

Over the next decade, they had a son, Walter in 1960. George continued touchups to the property, expanding the house and making a dedicated campsite for him and his family to enjoy on warm summer nights. Abigail expanded her garden work to simple, narrow paths that wound their way through the prettiest parts of the property.

In 1989, Abigail passed away peacefully, followed by George in 1991. Walter, not wanting to part with the house, but also not finding it convenient to live there, hired workers and staff to expand the trails and the lodgings further, taking his father's love of camping in the summer and converting it into an exclusive retreat, offering equipment and dedicated campsites with guided trail tours, bird watching and included meals.

In 2012, Walter's son and the current owner, Arthur, took over the resort. A bit of an outdoors enthusiast himself, Arthur expanded further on both the trails and the offered activities to include kayaking and yoga. He also took inspiration from a friend and had the dedicated campsites rebuilt with luxury yurts to truly bring the camping experience into the 21st century!
